Basic Vocab and Grammar

 

Word order is OSV (object-subject-verb), though the line between object and subject can be fuzzy at best. Compound words may be composed similarly to sentences, with parts of compound words being separated by short pauses in speech and apostrophes in writing, like so:

GHR QM KQH.

entropy I resist

"I am alive."

GHR'TR'KQH

entropy - that which - resists

organism(s); lit. "that which lives"

Word Meanings
QM I/me; we/us
QK you
QC they/them
QH someone/anyone
MSF water
GRK to grok; to reach Understanding with; to master; to love
KC to say; to talk; (as a noun) talk
SK to speak with; to converse with
HTR headspace; (mind)set; wherever a mind resides; (as a verb) to think
MM no; not; don't
SFS to surrender/yield to; to accept; to trust in; (as a noun) surrender/trust/acceptance
KQH to resist; (as a noun) resistance
MQS God; Mother Nature; the Universe; the universal Presence
GHR entropy; decay; (verb) to decay
FKG the "spirit"; the pattern which bears consciousness
GC this here; the following
QQC need; to need
HRT Truth; correctness; (verb) to be right
MC in/at/on
CM out of/beyond
HFT matter; (verb) to be tangible
TFH energy
MQCC interaction; (verb) to interact

Example sentence: "MQS QM SFS." ("I yield to God" or "I accept God's will").
