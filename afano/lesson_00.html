Polygraphs and other quirks

In cases where "ǧ" and "ž" are unavailable, the digraphs "gi" and "zi" replace them. In order to avoid ambiguity, "g'i" and "z'i" are used for /gi/ and /zi/ regardless of the availability of diacritics.

"f" is pronounced /f/ when at the beginning of a word or when found in an unvoiced consonant cluster. Elsewhere it is pronounced /v/. The exception is that "ff" is pronounced /f/ regardless of context.

"gn" may be pronounced /ŋ/ or /ɲ/. The two sounds are phonemically interchangeable.

"ae" is pronounced as either /ɛ/ or /æ/.
